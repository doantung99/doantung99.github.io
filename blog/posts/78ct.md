## Summary
This OSINT challenge handed over an aerial photo of a dragon-shaped park plus a story about a lake in the author's hometown. The solve was two-stage: geolocate the park to Ho dieu hoa Ho Son (Cong vien Ho Son) in Tuy Hoa, Phu Yen, Vietnam, then crack the text steganography in the lake-side message where abnormal characters spell a Base64 string.

## Solution

I pegged this as a geolocation-plus-cipher OSINT task the moment I saw an aerial shot with a "left behind" text blob. My job was steering; the model did the legwork.

1. **Geolocate the dragon.** I described the layout to the model — a dragon-shaped lake next to a circular "pearl" feature — and had it brainstorm Vietnamese search terms rather than English ones, since the story said "hometown." Queries like `công viên rồng nhả ngọc Phú Yên` and `hồ điều hòa hình rồng Tuy Hòa` pinned it to **Ho dieu hoa Ho Son / Cong vien Ho Son, Phuong 5, TP Tuy Hoa, Phu Yen, Vietnam**. I cross-checked the aerial silhouette against the image to confirm.

2. **Spot the steganography.** I fed the lake message to the model and asked it to explain why words like `traVelers`, `sh1mering`, and `lak3front` had weird capitalization and digits. It correctly identified the rule: in each token, take the one character that breaks the pattern — the lone uppercase in a lowercase word, the lone lowercase in an uppercase word, or any digit.

3. **Extract and decode.** I had the model write a solver, caught that the prefix `VjFU` decodes to `V1T` (a strong Base64 signal), and verified the final decode. One clean script from the raw message to the flag:

```python
#!/usr/bin/env python3
import base64
import re

text = """traVelers MAjESTIC reFlections sUrface LAKeSIDE sh1mering ScENERY AMAzING eXpanse lak3front glImmering BREEzES suNlit harGor ExPLORERS DREAMsCAPE LAKeVIEW riVerside 9 CALMsNESS harMor higHway hazY AMAzINGLY eXplore MAzEWORK BROAdWAY HARBoR sunMist tranTquil loVely OFfERS ARcHITECTS gleaminG MEADOwS harb0r skYline HAzE suNset 9"""

out = []
for word in re.findall(r"[A-Za-z0-9]+", text):
    digits = [c for c in word if c.isdigit()]
    lowers = [c for c in word if c.islower()]
    uppers = [c for c in word if c.isupper()]
    if digits:
        out.extend(digits)
    elif len(uppers) == 1 and len(lowers) > 1:
        out.append(uppers[0])
    elif len(lowers) == 1 and len(uppers) > 1:
        out.append(lowers[0])

b64 = ''.join(out)
print(b64)                                   # VjFUe1czX3IzNGxseV9sMHYzXzdoMTVfcGw0YzN9
print(base64.b64decode(b64).decode())        # V1T{W3_r34lly_l0v3_7h15_pl4c3}
```

The decode yields `V1T{...}`; normalizing the prefix to the challenge format `v1t{...}` gives the flag.
